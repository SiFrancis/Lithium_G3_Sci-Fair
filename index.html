The Hidden Air Cleaning Potential of Algae

Lithium - Group 3


Carbon dioxide is one of the most abundant greenhouse gases produced by human activity such as cars and factories. While it is produced naturally by humans and animals, too much of it traps heat in the atmosphere and speeds up global warming causing more devastation to the climate and the environment.

The most common method of slowing down this effect is by planting trees. This is because when plants photosynthesize, they absorb carbon dioxide in the air and turn them into oxygen that humans and animals can use to breathe. For this reason, plants and trees are sometimes nicknamed as “the lungs of the earth.”
